The system under test is a 5G Standalone (SA) core network where a gNB communicates with the Access and Mobility Management Function (AMF) over the N2 interface using NGAP over SCTP. 
UE registration procedures are initiated via NAS messages encapsulated in NGAP. Monitoring is performed on the 5G core side, observing control-plane signaling toward the AMF.

Protocol and interfaces:
- Interfaces: N2 (gNB <-> AMF)
- Protocols: SCTP, NGAP, NAS (5GS Mobility Management)

Property: The AMF must handle UE registration procedures in a stable manner and detect abnormal surges of registration signaling that may indicate a control-plane denial-of-service condition.

Trigger: One or more UEs initiate repeated NAS RegistrationRequest messages, encapsulated in NGAP InitialUEMessage or UplinkNASTransport, at a rate exceeding normal operational thresholds.

Expected message ordering (baseline):
Under normal conditions, a registration attempt follows this sequence:
1. InitialUEMessage (NGAP) carrying NAS RegistrationRequest
2. DownlinkNASTransport carrying NAS AuthenticationRequest
3. UplinkNASTransport carrying NAS AuthenticationResponse
4. Registration completion messages (e.g., RegistrationAccept, RegistrationComplete)

Observable Evidence:
The monitoring system must inspect:
SCTP: source/destination IPs and ports, association context, timestamps
NGAP: message type (InitialUEMessage, UplinkNASTransport), RAN-UE-NGAP-ID, AMF-UE-NGAP-ID, gNB identifier
NAS: presence of RegistrationRequest (when decodable)

Detected condition:
An alert must be raised when either:
- The number of RegistrationRequest messages exceeds a defined threshold within a time window (per gNB and/or per UE context), or
- repeated registration attempts are observed without completion of the expected registration procedure within a bounded time interval.

Response:
Upon detection, the system must generate an alert containing the time window, observed rate, affected identifiers (UE context and/or gNB), and the triggering NGAP/NAS message types, enabling correlation with AMF load and authentication failures.
